Privacy Policy.

Elementa is an offline-first educational web app. We collect no personal information, use no analytics, and set no advertising cookies. There is no account system.

What we store on your device

The app uses your browser's localStorage to keep your experience working between visits. The data never leaves your device.

  • Theme preference (dark or light)
  • Quiz best scores and daily-streak counter
  • Flashcard study schedule (spaced-repetition state)
  • Last viewed element and last selected trends configuration
  • One-time UI hint flags

You can clear all of this at any time by clearing your browser's site data for this domain.

Network requests

To render fonts and 3D libraries, your browser fetches files from these third-party CDNs the first time you load the app:

  • Google Fonts — Fraunces, Space Grotesk, JetBrains Mono.
  • unpkg.com — Three.js (atom & molecule rendering) and Chart.js (trends).

Those CDNs see your IP address and a standard user-agent string as part of any normal HTTP request. They do not receive any personal information from us. After the first load, the service worker caches these files locally and the app works offline.

Children

The app is suitable for students of any age. Because we collect no personal data, no parental consent flow is required.

Legal · Educational accuracy

Disclaimer.

Elementa is a learning aid, not an authoritative scientific reference.

Scientific accuracy

Numerical values (atomic masses, melting points, electronegativities, etc.) are sourced from public datasets and may contain rounding, transcription, or measurement errors. Different reputable sources sometimes disagree on the same value.

Not a textbook substitute

If you are preparing for an exam, writing a paper, or planning a real laboratory experiment, always cross-check values against your textbook, your instructor's notes, NIST, IUPAC, or another authoritative source.

Bonding simulator

The bonding lab uses a small curated table of common compounds. It is illustrative, not exhaustive. Real chemistry depends on conditions (temperature, pressure, catalysts) the simulator does not model. Do not use it to predict real-world reactions in a physical lab.

3D atomic models

The Bohr-style atom and ball-and-stick molecule visualisations are intentionally simplified. They are teaching aids and do not represent quantum-mechanical reality.
